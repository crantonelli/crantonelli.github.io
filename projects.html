Research Projects

Ecosystem Services

Weed seed predation by rodents

This project explored how native rodents contribute to weed control in the agroecosystems of central Argentina. We focus on herbicide-resistant species and how local variables affect predation rates.

Key Results: Predation is higher in high-quality field borders (borders with high vegetation coverage and richness), suggesting that maintaining natural vegetation can enhance this ecosystem service.
Foraging preferences

Seed traits and rodent choices

By conducting controlled experiments, we analyze how seed size, nutritional content, and chemical defenses influence the choices of different rodent species.

Key Results: Seed size is a primary driver for selection, but high protein content can override size preferences in certain seasonal contexts.
Environmental change

Urban occupancy of Mus musculus

We modeled the presence of the house mouse in expanding cities, considering urban growth patterns and socio-environmental variables.

Key Results: House mouse occupancy is strongly linked to the age of the urban sector and the density of built-up areas.
